PROJECT KAIĀRAHI



Project Kaiārahi is a solution developed to solve the current inefficiencies of the Auckland Council Disaster Recovery process. Disaster recovery is a long and arduous process, referring to the time following a disaster when the immediate danger has passed, and people are in need of assistance acquiring support and regaining what they have lost. The current method for referring these clients is a combination of Excel spreadsheets and Salesforce.

Project Kaiārahi ('kaiārahi' meaning 'guide') bridges the data gap between governments, clients, insurance companies, and charitable relief organisations. It does this through the use of AI suggestion and an improved database to speed up the decision-making process for government workers who contact and assist affected clients.

There are two different Figma prototypes that I developed following input from my team members and individual research. The first is 'advisor view', which is what the government worker sees. The second is 'client view', which is meant to be a minimal and easy-to-process form for clients to establish that they need assistance. These pages are designed to minimise the learning curve and for speed and ease of use.
