About Me



I’m originally from Hong Kong, and currently based in Auckland, New Zealand. I first discovered my love of computer science through my high school's coding club, which introduced students to the basics of Python. As the school had no official computer science course, I instead dedicated my time to competitions, hackathons, and self-teaching, until formally learning to code at UoA.

I decided to supplement my Computer Science experience with a major in Psychology, as I hold a keen interest on the ways human behaviour and technology can intersect. Through this, I have developed a strong interest in machine learning, and an insight into user-friendly design.

My ultimate goal is to make a meaningful contribution to the field of computer science.

Projects





Freecycle.org Phone Redesign

A Figma re-imagining of the current freecycle.org website as a portable app for tracking freecycling behaviours. This concept prototype was completed by me and my classmates Andrew Qiu and J.J. Jared, as a two-hour design project for our COMPSCI 345 (Human-Computer Interaction) course.





CCTV Band Website

This website was created as a personal project for my band, CCTV. I initially challenged myself to design a website within an hour based on the input of my band members, then decided to continue actually coding the website. The website is not yet live.





Pay-To-Win Kettle

For the 2023 End-Of-Year Terrible Ideas Hackathon, my team and I constructed the "Pay-To-Win" 'Amazon' Kettle. The goal of this 46-hour hackathon was to come up with and build a terrible idea. Ours was a kettle which requires you to listen to a full ad, without skipping, in order to use it.





To-Do List App

A mini-project done in my spare time while teaching myself React, this program allows you to create and modify a to-do list. Currently, the to-do list has functions for adding, removing, and marking tasks as complete. This project will have a drag feature added in the future.





Block Generator

A program which spawns different types of blocks with different travel paths, created as an Object-Oriented Programming exercise in my CS230 (Object-Oriented Software Development) course.





Summer Lab

I took part in Summer Lab 2024, which was a four-week program dedicated to identifying a problem, validating that problem, and developing a pitch for our solution. My team’s final showcase of our project, Project Kaiãrahi, took the form of a pitch presentation, for which I spoke and developed a Figma prototype based on our research.
